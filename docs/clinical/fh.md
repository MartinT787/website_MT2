# Profile: Cholesterol-Lowering Helper

**Patient-facing label:** Cholesterol-Lowering Helper
**Clinical name:** Familial Hypercholesterolemia (FH) and high-LDL nutrition support
**Internal ID:** `fh`

---

## Who this profile is for

People — kids, teens, and adults — with **persistently elevated LDL cholesterol** that didn't normalize on the general heart-healthy diet, with a focus on **familial hypercholesterolemia (FH)**.

This profile recognizes two clinical scenarios:

1. **Diagnosed FH** (HeFH or HoFH) — confirmed clinically (Dutch Lipid Clinic Network or Simon Broome criteria) or genetically.
2. **Severe primary hypercholesterolemia** — high LDL not yet labeled FH but high enough to need the same nutritional approach.

Both groups need **stronger LDL-lowering levers** than general heart-healthy eating provides.

### Lab thresholds for routing into this profile

| Population | LDL-C | Total cholesterol | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Adult (untreated) | ≥ 190 mg/dL | ≥ 290 mg/dL | Probable/possible FH, especially with family history of early heart disease. `[ref:nhlbi-2011]`, `[ext:nla-fh-2011]` |
| Adult (with risk factors) | ≥ 160 mg/dL | — | Add this profile alongside general. |
| Pediatric (2–19) | **≥ 160 mg/dL with family history of early heart disease** | — | Probable FH. `[ref:nhlbi-2011]`, `[ref:aap-2020]` |

### A note about medication

Most patients in this profile are also on a **statin** (and possibly ezetimibe, bempedoic acid, PCSK9 inhibitor, or evinacumab for HoFH). Diet does not replace medication for FH. Diet **adds to** medication for typically **5–15 % additional LDL reduction** when followed consistently. Surface this honestly.

## The framework: the LDL-lowering portfolio [`ext:nla-2014`, `ref:ucsf-adult`]

This profile stacks five evidence-based dietary levers. Each one alone is modest; together they can cut LDL by 20–30 %.

| Lever | Effect on LDL | How |
|---|---|---|
| Saturated fat < 7 % of calories | ↓ ~ 8–10 % | Swap butter, fatty meat, full-fat dairy, tropical oils for olive oil, fish, lean protein, low-fat dairy. |
| Dietary cholesterol < 200 mg/day | ↓ ~ 3–5 % | Limit egg yolks (≤ 2/week), shellfish portions, organ meats. |
| Plant sterols/stanols 2 g/day | ↓ ~ 6–10 % | Fortified spreads (Benecol, Smart Balance), fortified yogurt, supplements. |
| Viscous (soluble) fiber 5–10 g/day | ↓ ~ 3–5 % | Oats, barley, beans, lentils, psyllium, apples, citrus, eggplant, okra. |
| Soy protein ~ 25 g/day | ↓ ~ 3–5 % | Tofu, tempeh, edamame, soy milk. |
| (Bonus) Tree nuts 1 oz/day | ↓ ~ 3–7 % | Almonds, walnuts, pistachios. Calorie-dense — measure. |

This is sometimes called the **"portfolio diet"** (Jenkins et al.). The site presents it as **"Five LDL-lowering levers"** in patient language.

## Foods table (traffic light)

This builds on Heart-Healthy Eating — everything in that profile's "Eat freely" list still applies. The **changes** below are what makes this profile distinct.

### 🟢 Eat freely (FH emphasis)

The general-profile list, **plus** an explicit push on:

- **Oats** — 1 cup cooked daily (~ 2 g viscous fiber).
- **Beans and lentils** — ≥ ½ cup most days.
- **Barley** — substitute for rice when possible.
- **Psyllium** — 1 teaspoon in water or smoothie daily.
- **Eggplant, okra, apples, pears, oranges** — viscous fiber sources.
- **Almonds, walnuts** — 1 oz daily, raw or dry-roasted, unsalted.
- **Soy foods** — tofu, tempeh, edamame, plain unsweetened soy milk.
- **Plant-sterol-fortified products** — Benecol spreads, Smart Balance HeartRight, certain orange juices and yogurts. Read labels: target 2 g sterols/day total.

### 🟡 Be mindful

- **Egg yolks** — ≤ 2 per week (vs. 1/day in the general profile). Egg whites unrestricted.
- **Shellfish** — shrimp and squid are low in saturated fat but high in cholesterol; limit to 1 serving/week.
- **Lean red meat** — ≤ 1 serving/week (tighter than general).

### 🔴 Limit (FH emphasis)

The general-profile "Limit" list, **plus** especially strict on:

- **Butter, ghee, lard** — replace entirely with olive oil for cooking, plant-sterol spread for toast.
- **Coconut oil, palm oil, palm-kernel oil** — these are saturated fats, not "healthy" fats despite marketing.
- **Full-fat dairy** — switch to skim or 1 %.
- **Cheese** — minimize, even part-skim. ≤ 1 oz/week if possible.
- **Pastries, croissants, biscuits** — almost always made with butter or palm shortening.
- **Cream-based sauces, gravies** — at home and at restaurants.
- **Processed meats** — bacon, sausage, hot dogs.
- **Coconut-based "creamers" and ice creams** — same saturated-fat problem as dairy versions.

## Educational cards

1. **What is FH?** — One-paragraph explainer: a genetic condition (~ 1 in 250) where the body can't clear LDL well. Diet and medication together work.
2. **The five LDL-lowering levers** — Visual stack showing each lever and its rough % effect.
3. **Plant sterols 101** — What they are, why they work, where to find them, recommended dose.
4. **Viscous vs. insoluble fiber** — Both are good; viscous is the LDL-lowering kind.
5. **Statins and food** — Quick FAQ: do I still need to eat well? (Yes — ~ 5–15 % extra LDL reduction.) What about grapefruit? (Depends on which statin — check with pharmacist.)
6. **For families** — FH is autosomal dominant; first-degree relatives should be screened. Kids as young as 2 can have lipid panels.

## When to talk to your care team

> **Talk to your doctor or lipid specialist if:**
> - Your LDL hasn't dropped at all after 3 months of consistent eating changes.
> - You're considering stopping a statin because of cost or side effects.
> - You're pregnant, planning pregnancy, or breastfeeding — most lipid medications need to be paused.
> - You have new chest pain, shortness of breath, or pain in your calves/thighs.
> - A first-degree relative was just diagnosed with FH — get yourself and your kids screened.
